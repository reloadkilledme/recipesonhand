Food Safety

Below we will suggest best practices to keep you and your food safe to eat. For more information on food safety visit: https://www.fda.gov/food/buy-store-serve-safe-food/safe-food-handling

Make sure you keep everything clean; clean your hands, wash your food, if necessary, clean your working surfaces and use clean utensils. Keep food separate until cooked, avoid cross contaminating foods. Cook foods to proper temperatures (see Table 1.1). Keep refrigerated and freezer foods at temp until ready to cook.

Food Type: Internal Temperatures:
Beef, Pork, Veal, and Lamb 145°F with a 3-minute rest time
Ground Meat 160°F
Ham, Uncooked 145°F with a 3-minute rest time
Ham, Fully Cooked 140°F
Poultry 165°F
Eggs Cook until yolk & white are firm
Egg Dishes 160°F
Fin Fish 145°F or flesh is opaque & separates easily with fork
Shrimp, Lobster, and Crabs Flesh pearly & opaque
Clams, Oysters, and Mussels Shells open during cooking
Scallops Flesh is milky white or opaque and firm
Leftovers and Casseroles 165°F
Table 1.1 (FDA. 2022.)
